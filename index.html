About

A short introduction

I am a computer science undergraduate at New York University Abu Dhabi, with a minor in Economics and study-away experience in Paris and New York. My main research interest is how individually reasonable decisions can produce poor group outcomes, and whether AI systems can improve coordination in such settings.

My main project turns that question into a multi-party supply-chain simulation. It combines public-goods support, monitoring, remediation, and exit decisions in one repeated game, giving me a controlled way to study where coordination breaks down and which interventions might repair it.

I also work at eBRAIN Lab on Bio-LoRA, which uses biological priors to adapt large protein models efficiently. Although the domain is different, the standard is the same: make the mechanism explicit, test it against strong baselines, and check whether the result replicates.

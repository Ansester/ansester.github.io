About

Research approach

I am completing a B.S. in Computer Science at New York University Abu Dhabi, with study-away terms at NYU Paris and NYU New York. I work across the full empirical cycle: formulating a question, preparing data, developing a model or system, evaluating it, and communicating the result.

With Benjamin Rosche and Hanan Salam, I have completed a working paper specifying an online experiment on how AI-mediated interpretation and coordination affect collective action. The experiment is under development and combines game-theoretic modeling, behavioral measurement, and statistical analysis in a setting motivated by labor conditions in shared supply chains.

In the eBRAIN Lab, I work on parameter-efficient adaptation of ESM-2. My interest in this project is methodological: model training, efficient fine-tuning, benchmark design, sensitivity analysis, and determining whether observed improvements replicate across runs.

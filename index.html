About

Research profile

I am completing a B.S. in Computer Science at New York University Abu Dhabi, with study-away terms at NYU Paris and NYU New York. My main research area is human-AI collaboration, with a focus on social dilemmas in which individually rational behavior can undermine collective welfare.

With Benjamin Rosche and Hanan Salam, I am developing a repeated online experiment in which five human buyers interact with a supplier governed by a fixed decision rule. The design separates three functions that are often bundled in AI decision-support systems: strategic interpretation, normative interpretation, and support for coalition formation.

I also work in the eBRAIN Lab on Bio-Informed LoRA, a parameter-efficient method for introducing residue-level biological priors into ESM-2 adapters. My work across these projects emphasizes causal identification, appropriate baselines, and multi-seed evaluation.

Ashmit Mukherjee

Undergraduate researcher in cooperative AI and computational social science, studying how artificial agents change cooperation and coordination in groups of people and machines.

I am drawn to a specific failure: groups that would be better off cooperating, but don’t — not because anyone intends harm, but because responsibility is shared and each actor’s best move is to let someone else carry the cost. My main work asks whether AI agents, acting as advisors, coordinators, or monitors, can shift those outcomes. Alongside it I work on representation learning and evaluation in protein and multilingual models, where one question recurs everywhere I look: what counts as rigorous evidence that a result is real?

About

Academic Profile

I am a B.S. Computer Science student at New York University Abu Dhabi (minor in Economics, expected May 2027), with semesters at NYU Paris and NYU New York. My research focuses on learning and decision-making in structured systems: how models extract structure from complex data, and how that structure shapes behavior in multi-agent social systems and protein sequence modeling.

My current work centers on mixed human–AI populations — settings where people, institutions, and artificial agents jointly shape outcomes through coordination, monitoring, advice, and commitment. I approach these questions with simulation, game-theoretic modeling, and empirical evaluation; across every project, I care about benchmark design and what counts as evidence that an improvement is real.

Right now I am developing a capstone project on AI-mediated coordination in multi-party supply chain social dilemmas, advised by Prof. Hanan Salam and Prof. Benjamin Rosche, and working with eBRAIN Lab on biologically informed LoRA methods for protein language models.
